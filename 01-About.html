About

Mauricio Zambrano-Bigiarini is a Civil Engineer and PhD in Environmental Engineering, with more than 20 years of experience in water resources. In particular: development, implementation and assessment of hydrological models.

I’m currently working as a full-time professor at the Department of Civil Engineering of the Universidad de La Frontera, Temuco, Chile

Since March 2021, I’m working at the Assessment Group of Earth Sciences (Grupo de Evaluación de Ciencias de la Tierra) of the Chilean Science Foundation (ANID).

Current research interests

  • Use of satellite-based estimates of precipitation for drought monitoring and hydrological modelling.
  • Spatio-temporal analysis of droughts in data-scarce areas.
  • Global optimisation applied to hydrological models (PSO, DE, SCE-UA, SCEM, DREAM).
  • Global sensitivity analysis of hydrological models (Sobol’s, Morris).
  • Use of open-source tools for management and analysis of hydro-meteorological data (R, Qgis, Grass).
  • Development of R packages for hydrological analysis.

Education

  • 2007-2010: PhD in Environmental Engineering, Doctoral School in Environmental Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Università degli Studi di Trento, Trento, Italy.
    Research topic: “On the effects of hydrological uncertainty in assessing the impacts of climate change on water resources”
  • Nov-Dec 2002: Diploma in Water Pollution, Faculty of Physical and Mathematical Sciences, Universidad de Chile, Santiago, Chile.
  • Ago-Oct 2002: Diploma in Environmental Management, Faculty of Physical and Mathematical Sciences, Universidad de Chile, Santiago, Chile.
  • 1992-1998: Civil Engineer, Faculty of Engineering, University of Concepción, Concepción, Chile. Thesis title: Elaboración de un Programa en DELPHI para Modelación Unidimensional de Contaminantes en Ríos [Development of one-dimensional software modelling system for river pollutants, using Delphi language].
